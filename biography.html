Biography

Short Bio

    Wei Zhang is a Professor and Ph.D. supervisor at the School of Computer Science and Technology, East China Normal University, and a full-time mentor at Shanghai Chuangzhi College. He is a senior member of the China Computer Federation (CCF), an executive committee member of the CCF Database Technical Committee, and a committee member of the Social Media Processing Technical Committee of the Chinese Information Processing Society of China (CIPS). He received his Bachelor’s degree from the School of Computer Science and Technology, Harbin Institute of Technology, and his Ph.D. degree from the Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University, where his doctoral dissertation was recognized as an Excellent Doctoral Dissertation of Tsinghua University. During his doctoral studies, he was also a joint training Ph.D. student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. His long-term research interests include data mining, machine learning, recommender systems, large language models, and intelligent education. He has published over 100 papers in top international journals and prestigious academic conferences and has filed more than 10 national invention patents (7 granted). He has led key projects of the Ministry of Science and Technology, four projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China (including a major research plan cultivation project and general programs). He has received multiple honors, including the First Prize for Teaching Achievements from the Shanghai Computer Society, the Science and Technology Progress Award from the Shanghai Computer Society, and the ACM SIGMOD China Rising Star Award. He has also guided students to win championships and runner-up awards in several international top-tier conference competitions.
